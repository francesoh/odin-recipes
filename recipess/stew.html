STEW RECIPE

Description of Stew

Stew is a hearty and comforting dish enjoyed worldwide. It typically consists of ingredients like meat, vegetables, and aromatic seasonings
slow-cooked in a flavorful liquid, often broth or tomato-based sauce. Stews vary widely in taste and texture, offering endless possibilities for creativity in the kitchen,
making them a versatile and satisfying meal option.

Ingredients

  • 1 to 1.5 pounds of meat (beef, chicken, lamb, or pork), cubed
  • 2-3 tablespoons of cooking oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2-3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2-3 carrots, peeled and chopped
  • 2-3 potatoes, peeled and cubed (optional)
  • celery stalks, chopped (optional)
  • 1 can of diced tomatoes (14-15 ounces)
  • 4-5 cups of broth (chicken, beef, or vegetable)
  • Herbs and spices (bay leaves, thyme, rosemary, salt, and pepper)
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Procedure

  1. Brown the Meat:
    Heat the cooking oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the cubed meat and brown it on all sides. This adds flavor to the stew. Remove the meat and set it aside.

  2. Sauté the Onions and Garlic:
    In the same pot, add the chopped onions and minced garlic. Sauté them until they become soft and translucent.

  3. Add the Vegetables:
    Add the chopped carrots, potatoes (if using), and celery (if using) to the pot. Sauté them for a few minutes until they start to soften.

  4. Return the Meat:
    Put the browned meat back into the pot and stir to combine it with the vegetables.

  5. Add the Tomatoes:
    Pour in the can of diced tomatoes (including the juice) and stir well.

  6. Season the Stew:
    Add bay leaves, thyme, rosemary, salt, and pepper to the pot. Adjust the seasonings to your taste.

  7. Pour in the Broth:
    Pour the broth into the pot, making sure it covers the meat and vegetables. Adjust the amount of liquid based on your desired stew thickness.

  8. Simmer:
    Bring the stew to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let it simmer for 1-2 hours, or until the meat is tender and the flavors have melded. Stir occasionally.

  9. Taste and Adjust:
    Taste the stew and adjust the seasonings as needed. You can also add more liquid if the stew becomes too thick during cooking.

  10. Serve:
    Remove the bay leaves and serve your delicious stew hot. It's often enjoyed with bread, rice, or mashed potatoes. Feel free to customize your stew with your favorite vegetables and spices to create your own unique flavor profile
