JOLLOF RECIPE

Description of Jollofrice

Jollof rice is a vibrant and flavorful West African dish. It features parboiled rice cooked with tomatoes, onions, peppers, and a blend of spices. This one-pot delight is known for its vivid red or orange color and is often enriched with vegetables and protein like chicken or shrimp, making it a beloved and hearty meal.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of long-grain parboiled rice
  • 2-3 large tomatoes or 1 cup of tomato paste
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1-2 Scotch bonnet peppers or other hot peppers (adjust to your spice preference)
  • 1/2 cup of vegetable oil
  • 1 teaspoon of thyme
  • 1 teaspoon of curry powder
  • 2-3 bay leaves
  • 2-3 cups of chicken or vegetable stock (or water)
  • Salt to taste
  • Optional: 1-2 cups of diced vegetables (bell peppers, peas, carrots)
  • Optional: Protein of your choice (chicken, beef, shrimp, etc.)

Procedure

  1. Rinse the rice thoroughly under cold water until the water runs clear. Drain and set aside.

  2. Blend the Tomatoes and Peppers: If using fresh tomatoes, blend them with the Scotch bonnet peppers until you have a smooth tomato puree. If using tomato paste, skip this step.

  3. Sauté Onions: In a large pot or skillet, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until they become translucent.

  4. Add Tomato Sauce: Add the tomato puree (or tomato paste) to the pot. Stir and cook for about 10-15 minutes, allowing the tomatoes to reduce and the oil to separate from the sauce.

  5. Season the Sauce: Add thyme, curry powder, bay leaves, and salt to the tomato sauce. Stir well to incorporate the spices.

  6. Add Stock (or Water): Pour in the chicken or vegetable stock (or water) and stir. Bring the mixture to a simmer.

  7. Add Rice: Add the rinsed and drained rice to the pot. Stir to ensure the rice is well coated with the tomato sauce.

  8. Add Protein and Vegetables (Optional): If you're using protein (e.g., chicken, beef, or shrimp), add it to the pot at this point. Also, add diced vegetables if desired.

  9. Simmer and Cover: Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid, and let the Jollof rice simmer for about 20-30 minutes. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking, and add more stock or water if needed.

  10. Check for Doneness: The rice should absorb the sauce and become tender. Make sure the protein is fully cooked.

  11. Serve: Once the rice is cooked and the sauce has been absorbed, remove the bay leaves, fluff the rice with a fork, and serve your delicious Jollof rice hot.
